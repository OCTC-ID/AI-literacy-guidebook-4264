Introduction  ·  AI Literacy Framework
Why the BE Framework?

Artificial intelligence is changing how students learn, solve problems, and prepare for their future careers. New tools appear almost daily, bringing both opportunities and new questions. As faculty, we do not need to have every answer about AI—but we do have an important role in helping students develop the judgment they will need to use these tools thoughtfully and responsibly.

The BE Framework was created with that goal in mind.

Rather than focusing on specific AI tools or rapidly changing technologies, the framework focuses on five human behaviors that students can develop over time. These behaviors help students make informed decisions, act with integrity, think critically, and reflect on how AI influences their learning. Those habits will remain valuable long after today’s AI tools have evolved.

This playbook is designed to help faculty recognize where those behaviors already exist within their courses and provide practical ideas for strengthening them. You do not need to redesign your course or become an AI expert. In many cases, you are already teaching these habits—you simply have not been calling them AI literacy.

The Five BE Behaviors
Behavior Guiding Question
BE Safe Do I understand this tool well enough to use it wisely?
BE Honest Am I accurately representing my own work and AI use?
BE Critical Have I evaluated this information before trusting or using it?
BE Responsible Am I making intentional choices and taking ownership of the outcomes?
BE Reflective How is AI influencing my thinking, learning, and professional growth?

Together, these five behaviors provide a common language for discussing AI literacy across disciplines. Whether students are writing essays, troubleshooting engines, caring for patients, or designing business plans, the habits of thoughtful AI use remain remarkably consistent.

Learning Over Time

The BE Framework is developmental. Students are not expected to master these behaviors in a single assignment or even a single course. Instead, they build them through repeated practice, reflection, and coaching across their educational journey.

Introduction

Students become aware of the BE behaviors and begin recognizing them in their own learning and decision-making.

Exploration

Students practice the behaviors in a variety of course contexts with guidance, feedback, and opportunities to reflect.

Application

Students apply the behaviors independently and transfer their judgment to new tools, situations, and professional contexts.

Progress matters more than perfection. The goal is not flawless AI use—it is increasingly thoughtful decision-making.
A Framework for Faculty

The BE Framework is a coaching resource, not a grading system. Its purpose is to help faculty notice where AI literacy naturally appears in their teaching and make those moments more intentional.

Every discussion about evaluating evidence, documenting sources, reflecting on learning, or making ethical decisions is already an opportunity to build AI literacy. This playbook provides a shared language and practical strategies for supporting that work.

You do not need to introduce all five behaviors at once. Begin with the behavior that connects most naturally to your course, assignment, or current instructional challenge.

About the Framework
Framework Foundation

The BE Framework was developed by the OCTC AI Workgroup as a behavioral interpretation of the Digital Education Council (DEC) AI Literacy Framework. It translates broad AI literacy competencies into observable student behaviors that can be integrated naturally into teaching and learning across disciplines. Additional information about the DEC framework and its relationship to the BE Framework is included in Appendix A.

This guide supports the OCTC AI Literacy Framework, a student-centered interpretation of the Digital Education Council AI Literacy Framework.
